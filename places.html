Yellowstone

Enjoy a day in Yellowstone National Park!

On March 1, 1872, Yellowstone became the first national park for all to enjoy the unique hydrothermal and geologic features. Within Yellowstone's 2.2 million acres, visitors have unparalleled opportunities to observe wildlife in an intact ecosystem, explore geothermal areas that contain about half the world's active geysers, and view geologic wonders like the Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone River.

Tetons

Take a beautiful hike through the tetons!

Rising above a scene rich with extraordinary wildlife, pristine lakes, and alpine terrain, the Teton Range stands as a monument to the people who fought to protect it. These are mountains of the imagination. Mountains that led to the creation of Grand Teton National Park where you can explore over two hundred miles of trails, float the Snake River, and enjoy the serenity of this remarkable place.

Coeur D'Alene

Stay a night at the Coeur D'Alene Resort!

Coeur d’Alene is a city in northwest Idaho. It’s known for water sports on Lake Coeur d’Alene, plus trails in the Canfield Mountain Natural Area and Coeur d’Alene National Forest. McEuen Park offers a grassy lawn and a trailhead for adjacent Tubbs Hill. The lakeside City Park & Beach includes picnic areas and a playground.

Boise

Check out the State Capitol!

Boise is the capital city of Idaho. The Boise River Greenbelt is a series of tree-dotted trails and parks hugging the water's edge. In an art deco building, downtown’s Boise Art Museum has contemporary works and an outdoor sculpture garden. The grand, sandstone Idaho State Capitol Building is nearby.

Redfish Lake Lodge

Take a trip to the Sawtooth Mountains!

Redfish Lake is an alpine lake in Custer County, Idaho, just south of Stanley. It is the largest lake within the Sawtooth National Recreation Area.

With plenty of hiking, fishing, backpacking, and the beautiful natural beach you will have plenty to do!

Stanley

Stay in Stanley while you raft!

This little town sits at the base of the Sawtooth Mountains and is surrounded by over 1000 miles of untouched wilderness. With plenty of hotels and places to camp you will definitely enjoy your time in this cute little town with a great view the whole time!

Albion

Visit this little historic town!

Head down to Albion in Southern Idaho, just an hour away from Shoshone Falls, anytime of the year. Winter if you want to cross country ski or take out a snowmobile. Fall for their awesome haunted houses and Halloween celebration. Spring and Summer for great views and plenty of outdoor activities

Diamond Peak

Go on a brutal hike!!

Sitting at just above 12,000ft, Diamond Peak is the 3rd highest peak in Idaho and is about 35 miles East of Mount Borah. This 6.5 mile out-and-back hike will give you a great view and even better workout
